Why it beats raw prompting

The guardrails an AI content workflow actually needs.

  • Money moves only with consent. Every project has a budget cap and spend ledger; the agent shows estimated cost before generating and previews cheap before rendering expensive.
  • Consistency is engineered, not hoped for. Characters, products, logos, and voices are generated once, locked in an asset registry, and reused by ID — the same face across a whole campaign.
  • Text never garbles. Slides and reels get pixel-perfect typography rendered locally with real fonts (Remotion), for $0.
  • It ships upload-ready. Correctly sized media per platform, captions within char limits, hashtags, and alt text — not a folder of loose renders.
  • Proof stays real. The agent is hard-blocked from inventing testimonials, stats, or press. Your numbers, verbatim, or nothing.

How it works

You describe it in plain English. The agent plans, prices, generates, and assembles — you just approve.

You Make a 30-second cinematic story film for my coffee brand ☕
Claude On it — running pre-flight first. What's the brand name, who's it for, and your budget cap?
You Brooke's Coffee, for busy founders. Cap it at $5.
Claude Got it. Here's the plan — estimated $3.65, under your cap. Building now: ↳ createProject brookes-coffee/ + brand.md ↳ generateImage founder keyframe → locked as char-founder ↳ registerAsset char-founder → assets.yaml (reused every scene) ↳ writeScript 4-scene story + adaptive narration ↳ generateVideoFromImage ×4 Veo 3.1 clips, same face ↳ generateVoiceover per-scene delivery ↳ assembleFinal concat clips + VO + music → final.mp4
Claude Done. 32-second film assembled, cost $3.62, logged to your ledger. Packaged 9:16 + 16:9, captions and hashtags included — ready to upload. ✅
